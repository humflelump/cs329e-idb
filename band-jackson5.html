The Jackson Five

The band originally consisted of 3 elder brothers of the Jackson Family, Jackie, Tito, and Jermaine Jackson, but the group took off after the addition of the youngest brothers, Marlon and Michael Jackson. The Jackson Five was one of the first black groups to obtain a crossover following. When the band switched from Motown Records to Epic Record, Jermaine Jackson left the group and was replaced by youngest Jackson brother, Randy Jackson. The band is well known for it lead vocalist, Micheal Jackson, who career skyrocketed after his departure from the group in 1984. Since then the group has been a part of American pop culture, influencing music for generations. Since their origin, the band has inducted in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and received a great amount of accolades.
